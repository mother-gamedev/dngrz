# DNGRZ — World & Identity Bible (Phenoms, Schools, Powers)

**Date:** 2026-05-25 (rev. 2 — post adversarial review by lore-builder + creative-catalyst)
**Parent spec:** `docs/superpowers/specs/2026-04-12-dngrz-game-design.md`
**Status:** Creative/identity design. Locks the world frame, the tonal spine, the premise,
the 8 Power-Schools (re-souled), the school wheel, the 4-layer power kit, the Called Shot,
Crowd-as-Territory, and the Captain model. Full roster + combo catalog are the follow-up (§15).

> **This document is the canonical world-and-identity source, and it SUPERSEDES master-spec
> §9 ("Setting & Tone").** §9 committed to a *pure original-fantasy* universe (elemental
> "fire-mage" factions, "ignore the lore entirely"). That stance is retired. The setting is a
> **heightened alternate Earth where legends are forged by playing DNGR Ball**, and lore/identity
> is a **design pillar**, not optional flavor. The master spec needs an editor's note pointing
> here (see §15). This bible changes none of the master spec's *mechanical chassis* — Phenom
> slots, synergy tiers, draft/constructed structure, momentum, zones — it gives that chassis a
> soul and upgrades the ability language.

## 1. Purpose & DNA

The master spec built a strong *skeleton* (Phenom ability slots; 2/4/6-piece faction synergy;
ban+snake draft; 9/4/3 constructed; momentum; field zones) but left the *soul* TBD. The first
draft of this bible supplied a competent taxonomy that an adversarial review correctly called
**generic** — a familiar "myths waking" premise bolted to baseball, a reskinned elemental
wheel, hand-wavy stakes, and an essentialism risk in how it sourced real cultures. This
revision fixes that at the root.

**Three-way DNA, kept in balance:** Baseball (the foundation — fun as a ball game first) +
Mario Superstar Baseball (chargeable in-the-moment specials, captains) + Magic: The Gathering
(schools-as-colors, synergy deckbuilding, draft, captain-as-commander). The deckbuilding
*seasons* the baseball; it never replaces it.

**Naming:** the game/title is **DNGRZ**; the in-world sport is **DNGR Ball** — read "Dinger
Ball" (dingers = home runs), with "danger ball" as the stylish second read.

## 2. Tonal Spine (pick one, commit)

> **DNGRZ feels like the last day of summer that mattered more than it should have —
> earnest, heightened sports-anime glory-chasing wrapped in folkloric stadium magic.**
> *Blue Lock / Haikyū sincerity × Field of Dreams chalk-dust myth × a fighting game's snap.*

Everything — UI sound, stadium light, a Signature's framing, a Phenom's name — serves that one
feeling. The other influences are **demoted to accents, not co-tones:**

- **Valorant** → *visual/production* reference only (clean, premium, real-world fashion). Not a tone.
- **MSB** → *moment-to-moment juice* reference (specials feel snappy and fun). Not the world tone.
- **Mythology** → supplies *weight at the climaxes* (Signatures, Called Shots), not the baseline.

This resolves the "four-way grab-bag" problem: one spine, three accents.

## 3. Premise — "Legends Are Forged by Play"

The reverence is gone. Power does **not** flow from ancient gods into chosen ones. Instead:

**Belief mints legend; declaration claims it; the field forges it.** In this alternate Earth,
DNGR Ball is the planet's defining sport, and it has become *culturally massive enough that its
play writes the world's living mythology in real time.* A rookie who calls her shot in a World
Classic final and delivers doesn't channel a storm god — she *becomes* one, and seasons later
the next generation will channel **her**. The "old myths" are simply earlier legends, sport-
saints whose feats calcified into archetypes that new players grow into and reshape.

This single reversal does a lot of work the old premise couldn't:

- **It earns the baseball.** Myth is *made by playing*, so the sport isn't a metaphor for the
  power — it is the *only* thing that generates and ranks it. Strip the baseball and the world
  ceases to exist. That's ownable.
- **It defuses the appropriation risk.** Power is self-generated by the league and its
  cultures, not borrowed from living religions. (See §14.)
- **It's forward-looking and live-service-native.** The roster is a *growing canon you write
  by playing.* This season's breakout becomes next season's myth-pool — perfect for seasonal content.

### The crucible, the stakes, the governance (no more hand-waving)

- **What's at stake — and it's personal.** You play for *who you become.* Every game writes
  (or fails to write) your legend into the world's living canon; the crowd, the record, the
  myth-pool are the real prizes the ladder only stands in for. This is a glory-chase, not a
  death-sport — the warmth of §2 always wins ties.
- **Why a regulated field, not a brawl?** A legend half-formed is *restless* — un-channeled, it
  leaks into the world (a storm that won't quite disperse, a slump that follows you home). The
  **ruled diamond is the crucible**: ninety feet, the count, three outs — the structure that
  lets a Phenom *spend and shape* that power cleanly. The field is release valve and forge both.
- **Who keeps it?** The **League** sanctions play and keeps the records that *are* the
  mythology — part stage, part steward. Deliberately a *light institutional hum* behind the
  personal story, never a looming authority; if the lore ever drifts toward dystopia, it has
  overcorrected.

## 4. Grouping Architecture — Three Axes, One Spine

Three influences each wanted to "group" Phenoms (MTG colors, MSB captains, real nationalities).
They live on **three separate axes** so none buries the others:

| Axis | What it is | What it drives | Avoids |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Power-School** | A *legend-archetype* — a way to become immortal in DNGR Ball (8 of them) | **Mechanical synergy** (2/4/6-piece), combos, zone types | — |
| **Nationality** | Where a Phenom is from; their nation's DNGR Ball folklore | **Identity & lore**; light "homeland" flavor; World-Classic framing | "nationality = stat" essentialism; mono-nation lineups |
| **Captain** | A designated keystone Phenom | **Deck identity**: school primary, Command aura, marquee Signature | flat, focus-less rosters |

**The spine:** *Schools carry the synergy, nationality carries the soul, one Captain anchors the
deck.* Synergy is about **playstyle**, never passport.

## 5. The Eight Power-Schools (re-souled as legend-archetypes)

Each school is now **a kind of DNGR Ball legend you can become** — grounded in the alt-world's own
sport folklore, with mythic resonance as *flavor*, not borrowed scripture. Each still owns a
**baseball pillar**, maps to a **tempo archetype**, and holds a **wheel slot** (§6).

**Controlled asymmetry (the fix for "too tidy"):** the schools are deliberately *not*
symmetric. Tide is over-represented (the meta's villain). Shade has no clean weakness but pays
costs. Storm is a true double-weakness glass cannon. Dawn is a glass-jaw king. Variety in the
*shape* of strength/weakness is what makes them feel like different games to play.

### ⚡ STORM — Power *(Aggro)* — "The Slugger"
- **Legend:** the one whose dingers shook the deck and whose name the bleachers chant — the
  moonshot, the broken scoreboard, the called-shot bomber.
- **Plays like:** explosive power, blazing velocity, momentum in bursts off extra-base hits.
- **Weakness *(double, by design)*:** erratic contact *and* fragile to disruption — wins in
  three innings or implodes in one. The purest glass cannon.

### 🌊 TIDE — Pitching & Control *(Control)* — "The Ace" — *META VILLAIN*
- **Legend:** the untouchable arm that made hitters look foolish — the unhittable pitch, the
  painted black, the ice-cold closer who never blinked.
- **Plays like:** big-breaking movement, "current" zones that slow/redirect batted balls,
  patience, *denies* enemy momentum, wins late.
- **Asymmetry — the villain the meta *discovers*:** leans strongest (real baseball is
  pitching-dominant), so high-level play organizes around *"how do you beat Tide?"* — a game
  needs a villain, and perfect balance has none. **But tuned at launch so newcomers don't just
  lose to nonsense before they know the meta exists:** the oppression is a ceiling players grow
  into, not a launch-day wall.
- **Weakness:** almost no burst power — if forced to chase, it has no gear for it.

### 🔨 FORGE — Structure & the Rulebook *(Midrange / cerebral)* — "The Gamesmith"
- **Legend:** *recast from "defense" to the master of the game's own structure* — the smith as
  lawgiver. The double-play architect, the rally-killer, the rulebook lawyer, the perfectly
  executed squeeze, the manager-legend who always had the right card.
- **Plays like:** manipulates the count, forced outs, situational rules, and ironclad defense;
  "tempered" buffs that compound; brutally consistent. The scariest *cerebral* school, not a grind.
- **Weakness:** rigid — chaos and disruption (Beast tempo, Trickster bluffs) punish its scripts.

### 🐾 BEAST — Speed *(Tempo)* — "The Phantom"
- **Legend:** too fast to throw out — the inside-the-park homer, the wall-climbing robbery, the
  steal of home, the blur on the basepaths.
- **Plays like:** baserunning, stealing, huge fielding range, reflex "pounce" catches, momentum off speed.
- **Weakness:** light bats; a step behind on raw power.

### 🃏 TRICKSTER — Disruption *(Combo / mind-game)* — "The Hustler"
- **Legend:** the bush-league genius who wins with mind games — the hidden-ball trick, the
  fake-to-third, the deke, the (folkloric) sign-stealing caper.
- **Plays like:** disguised pitches, scrambles opponent reads, steals info, debuffs and bluffs,
  wrecks enemy zone setups.
- **Weakness:** feeble raw stats — a combo engine, useless without setup.

### 🌿 VERDANT — Sustain & Ramp *(Engine / combo)* — "The Dynasty"
- **Legend:** the one who builds — the farm-system patriarch, the late-bloomer, the home-grown
  core that compounds across a long season into something unkillable.
- **Plays like:** buffs that *grow* each inning, healing/sustain, momentum engine, "bloom" zones
  that expand, late-game payoff.
- **Weakness:** slow start — run over by aggro before it stabilizes.

### ☠️ SHADE — Curse & Comeback *(Attrition / comeback combo)* — "The Ghost"
- **Legend:** *re-souled away from death-gods toward baseball's own ghost stories* — the cursed
  franchise, the haunted stadium, the goat, the ghost runner, the player written off who refuses
  to stay buried and breaks the hex.
- **Plays like:** spends resources (momentum, bench Phenoms, even outs) for big payoffs; *recurs*
  spent abilities; lingering "hexes" (debuffs); **gets stronger the further behind it falls** —
  the home for the spec's trailing-team rally mechanic.
- **Asymmetry:** no clean stat weakness — instead it *pays costs* to function. On a clean, even
  board with nothing to spend and no deficit to exploit, it stalls.

### ☀️ DAWN — Leadership & Spectacle *(Support / go-wide)* — "The Franchise Hero"
- **Legend:** the beloved icon whose presence lifts the whole roster — the clubhouse leader, the
  walk-off hero the city worships, the captain everyone plays harder for.
- **Plays like:** team-wide auras, ally buffs, momentum-steadying, the **captain-iest** school;
  its light "burns away" Trickster disguises.
- **Weakness *(sharpened — glass-jaw king)*:** its auras are *public and targetable* — a Trickster
  disruption or a sudden crowd-flip can collapse the whole team buff at once. Lead the parade or
  lose it all; not "mildly weak alone."

**Pillar coverage:** power, pitching, structure, speed, disruption, sustain, curse/comeback,
leadership. Schools own the master-spec zone types (Tide = frost/current, Storm = blaze/spark,
Verdant = bloom…) and have minor secondary reach, MTG-color-pie style.

## 6. The School Wheel — Four Opposed Axes (with intentional asymmetry)

Eight schools, four opposed axes (an octagon). **Adjacent = allies** (easy splashes); **opposed
= enemies** (spicy, high-skill combos). But the wheel is *not* perfectly balanced — see §5
asymmetry. Players should *learn* the meta (Tide is oppressive; Dawn is high-variance), not read
a symmetric chart and feel nothing.

| Axis | Pole | ↔ | Pole |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Tempo** | ⚡ Storm (slugger / power) | ↔ | 🌊 Tide (ace / control) |
| **Body** | 🐾 Beast (phantom / speed) | ↔ | 🔨 Forge (gamesmith / structure) |
| **Mind** | 🃏 Trickster (hustler / deception) | ↔ | ☀️ Dawn (franchise hero / clarity) |
| **Cycle** | 🌿 Verdant (dynasty / growth) | ↔ | ☠️ Shade (ghost / curse-comeback) |

Thematic counters are mechanical: **Dawn**'s light burns **Trickster** disguises; **Verdant**'s
growth outlasts **Shade**'s decay; **Tide** quells **Storm**; **Forge**'s structure cages
**Beast**'s chaos (and inversely Beast's chaos cracks Forge's scripts).

*(Expansion candidate, deliberately not in launch 8 to respect "keep the wheel": a ninth
**"Unwritten"** school — Phenoms whose legend hasn't settled yet, no wheel opposite, splashable
into any deck. It fits the premise perfectly and is the obvious symmetry-breaker if the meta
ever needs a wildcard. Parked in §15.)*

## 7. The Power Kit — Four Layers, Three Fuels

Every Phenom has a **4-layer kit**. The master spec gave three slots
(passive/tactical/signature); this adds the **Special** — the chargeable in-the-moment
pitch/swing/catch that is the MSB heartbeat.

| Layer | Fuel | When | Feel |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Trait** | always on | passive | school identity ("extra-base hits build momentum") |
| **Special** ⭐ | personal **Charge** meter (earned by good play) | live, action phase | the MSB special pitch / swing / catch — frequent spice |
| **Tactic** | tactical clock / tactic resource | tactical phase | zones, buffs, disruption — the strategy layer |
| **Signature** | full crowd (see §9) | live, climactic | the Final-Smash highlight-reel ultimate |

### Anti-overload rule (the review's valid worry)

Three economies × nine Phenoms could become bookkeeping that fights the "accessible arcade
baseball" floor. So the layers are **separated in time and attention** by design:

- **In the live action phase the player actively manages exactly ONE meter: Charge.**
- **Tactic resource is spent only in the (paused) tactical phase** — never juggled live.
- **The crowd (momentum) is ambient** — you *feel* it, you don't manage a bar.

**Worked at-bat (the proof):** You're batting. The crowd-lean is ambient — you can hear it. Your
eyes are on the pitch and your one live meter, Charge. You've banked enough to fire your Special
(a power swing); you decide *now or hold*. You don't touch tactic resources (those set your
zones during the last pause) and you can't fire your Signature (the crowd isn't fully yours).
If you're bold, you tap **Call Shot** first (§8), then swing. One read, one resource, one
optional declaration. Everything else is ambient or already decided.

The casual↔competitive lever is concrete: **a casual player ignores Charge and swings on
instinct — and the game is tuned so instinct-swinging is genuinely viable arcade fun — while a
competitive player treats Charge as a frame-tight economy.** Same input, two resolutions. That
is what "progressive depth" actually means here.

## 8. The Called Shot (signature mechanic / hook #1)

The most mythic real moment in baseball — Babe Ruth pointing to the fence — becomes a core,
ownable verb, and it *is the premise made playable*: **you author your legend out loud.**

- **Declare:** before a pitch, a batter (or pitcher) can spend to **Call the Shot** — name an
  outcome ("home run, left field" / "strikeout looking").
- **Deliver → enormous payoff:** a big swing in crowd-territory toward you (§9), Charge/Signature
  progress, and it literally *writes legend* (records, a permanent mark, season-canon).
- **Fail → public penalty:** crowd flips toward your opponent, Charge loss, and a visible
  "called-and-missed" brand for the rest of the at-bat.
- **Gated, not spammed:** Called Shots cost a real resource and/or are limited per game, so each
  is a *moment*, not a habit. This keeps it a spice/overlay, not a fourth meter.

It's a bluff/commit duel layer on top of the pitch-vs-bat read, it's intrinsically dramatic, and
it's the shot you build the trailer around: *"He CALLED it."*

## 9. Crowd-as-Territory (momentum, reimagined / hook #2)

The master spec's momentum tug-of-war becomes **spatial, visible, and contestable — because in
this world, belief is literally what mints legend.**

- The stadium is divided into **crowd sections** you hold or lose. The proportion you hold *is*
  your momentum level.
- Hits, robberies, Signatures, and delivered Called Shots **visibly flip sections** to your side
  — the wave, the chant, the color change ripple across the stands.
- **Full crowd** (you hold (nearly) the whole stadium) is what unlocks **Signatures** (§7).
- **Trailing-team rally (per spec):** the crowd naturally drifts toward the underdog, giving the
  information advantages the master spec already specifies — now diegetic.
- **Shade** can *steal* the home crowd outright; the meme writes itself: *"I lost the game but I
  took their whole stadium."* Casters narrate crowd-territory like a campaign map.

This makes the most spectator-legible system in the game (you can *see and hear* who's winning)
also the metaphysical engine of the world. No competitor welds "the audience is the battlefield"
to a sport.

## 10. Captains — the Keystone Model

Captains fuse MTG-Commander build-around with MSB "the captain *is* the team," and in this world
a Captain is **a legend other Phenoms orbit.**

- A subset of Phenoms are **Captain-class** — marquee legends; the prestige/collection
  centerpiece and the lore handle for teams (a club or national side is known by its captain).
- You designate **one Captain** per roster: they grant a **Command aura** (team-wide,
  school-flavored) *and* own your marquee **Signature**.
- **Keystone constraint:** the Captain's school is your **primary**. Same-school and
  **allied-school** (adjacent) Phenoms get full synergy; **opposed-school** splashes are
  weakened. "Who's your captain?" is *the* deckbuilding question, and the wheel governs which
  splashes are easy vs. spicy.

### Mode behavior (constructed / draft / esport)

The whole system is **format-agnostic**; only the Captain needs mode-specific handling:

- **Constructed:** full keystone build-around — the deep end.
- **Draft:** the captain *emerges* from your picks; landing a Captain-class Phenom is a priority
  "bomb" pick; commit-early-vs-stay-open is the draft tension. **Any Phenom can run as a weaker
  field captain** so you're never captainless (preserves "draft is the great equalizer").
- **Esport:** supports a mixed tournament (e.g., Swiss constructed → draft top-cut) *or* separate
  ladders, no system changes.

## 11. Example Phenom Kits (illustrative)

Demonstrating the language across both sides of the duel — and giving each a **want + rival**, so
they're characters, not effect-lists (the "no soul" fix; rivalries are the unit of story).
Names/stats are placeholders for the roster pass (§15). Note the deliberate avoidance of
"[nationality] = [god]": these are *people* whose play echoes a legend.

**⚡ Raio — Brazil — Power Hitter / CF — (Storm)**
- *Want / rival:* desperate to drag his favela sandlot's name into the world canon; estranged
  from Lin, who left the same barrio for the polished pro academy.
- **Trait — Overcharge:** extra-base hits give bonus crowd; with 2 strikes his swings hit harder.
- **Special — Thunderclap:** power swing with a visible wind-up tell; clean contact ignores the
  first defensive zone it crosses. Brutal whiff if mistimed.
- **Tactic — Spark Zone:** outfield-gap zone; balls through it gain carry.
- **Signature — Tempestade:** next swing is a guaranteed launch; if gone, clears the bases and
  takes the whole stadium.

**🌊 Lin, the Tidecaller — Taiwan — Control Pitcher — (Tide)**
- *Want / rival:* believes legend is *earned by craft*, not noise; Raio is everything she
  disciplined out of herself.
- **Trait — Undertow:** opponent crowd-gains are reduced while she pitches.
- **Special — Riptide:** a pitch that breaks *twice*; whiffing it drains the batter's Charge.
- **Tactic — Current Zone:** slows batted balls through it (kills line drives).
- **Signature — Deluge:** rest of the at-bat, every pitch bends in flight and the batter's read-
  window fogs.

**🃏 Kọ́lá, the Eight-Legged — Nigeria — Disruptor / 2B — (Trickster)**
- *Want / rival:* a streetball folk-hero who thinks the League is rigged for the polished, and
  delights in humiliating Forge "rulebook" types.
- **Trait — Misdirection:** your pitch-type tell is hidden on the first pitch of every at-bat.
- **Special — Shell Game:** instantly swap with an adjacent fielder after the pitch, scrambling
  the batter's read.
- **Tactic — False Zone:** place a decoy zone that looks real but does nothing.
- **Signature — Long Con:** steal and reveal one enemy hidden zone, and scramble two of their fielders.

## 12. Example Combos & Synergies (illustrative)

The wheel governs combo *difficulty*: allied combos are accessible; opposed combos are high-skill,
high-payoff. Full catalog is the §15 follow-up.

- **Storm + Beast — "Snowball" (allied aggro):** Beast steals → Storm's *Overcharge* converts the
  crowd swing into a power buff → relentless pressure.
- **Tide + Storm — "Tempest" (opposed):** Tide denies enemy crowd all game while Storm hoards
  Charge → one Signature swings the whole match. Hard, enormous payoff.
- **Verdant + Forge — "Fortress" (allied engine):** growing buffs + structural rule-locks compound
  into an unbreakable mid-game. Win by attrition.
- **Shade + Trickster — "Hex" (allied combo):** Shade falls behind on purpose to power up →
  Trickster recycles a Tactic and scrambles reads → you steal the crowd and bleed them out.

## 13. Mode Considerations (carry-forward)

Per master-spec §7 there are **Constructed** and **Draft** modes, with **esport** play potentially
mixing both. Every system here serves both identically; only the Captain has a mode rule (the
draft field-captain fallback, §10). The casual↔competitive spread is delivered through
*progressive depth from one ruleset* (§7), not separate modes: ignore captains/wheel/Charge/
Called-Shot and it's arcade baseball; engage them and it's a deckbuilding esport.

## 14. Representation Principles (non-negotiable, strengthened)

The first draft's §11 had good intent but its school tables violated it. These are the binding rules:

1. **Legend, not deity; person, not type.** Power is a *self-forged DNGR Ball legend* with mythic
   resonance — not a borrowed god. Phenoms are their own characters with names, regions, and
   stories. **The "-kind" suffix is banned** ("Shàngó-kind" turns a person into a type-token of a
   god). Lead with the person; let resonance be discovered (the Lin example does this right).
2. **Universal archetype, local face — but never "the [nation] storm-slot."** Schools are
   legend-archetypes; nationalities are specific, varied, dignified expressions of that nation's
   *DNGR Ball* culture and folklore.
3. **No essentialism in mechanics.** Nationality never sets stats or synergy. **Acceptance test
   (write it during the roster pass, before shipping any nation):** name three Phenoms from the
   same nation in *three different schools/roles/personalities.* If "the Nigerian one" is always
   a Trickster, you built the stereotype you swore off.
4. **Tier your sourcing by liveness.** Build each school's spine from *original, synthesized*
   legend (our invention) and the alt-world's DNGR Ball folklore. Draw faces predominantly from
   **myth-as-literature** (dead-religion canon: Norse, Greek, Egyptian). Use **living, practiced
   faiths sparingly, specifically, and only with named, paid practitioner consultants** — never
   as one-of-four interchangeable faces. Do not overload any single living tradition (the first
   draft leaned on Yoruba òrìṣà across four schools — corrected).
5. **Shade is curses-and-comebacks, not a religion's afterlife.** It draws on *baseball's own
   ghost stories* (cursed franchises, haunted stadiums, the comeback that breaks the hex), NOT
   Vodou/Egyptian/Aztec death-deities. (Baron Samedi / Anubis / Mictlāntēcutli name-drops from
   the first draft are pulled.) If any future Phenom touches a living afro-diasporic tradition,
   it is built with deep consultation as a *dignified* figure or not at all.
6. **Breadth is a feature.** The roster visibly spans continents, body types, ages, genders. A
   design pillar, not a checkbox.

**Relationship to the implementation track:** This is a *creative/identity* bible. It feeds future
implementation plans (an ability/kit system, school-synergy resolution, the Charge/crowd economy,
Called Shot, captain auras) but is **not** itself the next code plan — the active code track is the
deterministic-core → playable-duel → decision-layer sequence. This bible is the canon those later
systems are flavored and balanced against.

**Discipline — canon is frozen here until the duel earns its keep.** This bible is a promissory
note drawn against a core pitch/bat loop that *failed Gate 1* and is still being reproven. Do
**not** deepen the canon (roster, combos, new systems) until the bare duel is fun in playtest;
the lore cannot rescue a duel that doesn't feel good. And treat §7's "one live meter" anti-overload
rule as a **hypothesis** — prototype it early on a *full inning of two interacting kits*, not as
the last thing validated in the roster pass. The worked at-bat is one swing in isolation; the real
test is interaction.
